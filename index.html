A Modern Guide to an Ancient Philosophy

What is Stoicism?

Stoicism is a philosophy of personal ethics informed by its system of logic and its views on the natural world. It teaches the development of self-control and fortitude as a means of overcoming destructive emotions, focusing on what we can control and accepting what we cannot with grace.

Core Principles

The Dichotomy of Control

Focus your energy only on what you can control: your judgments, impulses, and actions. Accept everything else with tranquility.

Memento Mori

"Remember you will die." This isn't morbid; it's a tool to create priority and meaning, reminding us to live each day with purpose.

Amor Fati

"A love of one's fate." Embrace everything that happens in life, including adversity, as an opportunity to practice virtue and grow.

Premeditatio Malorum

The premeditation of evils. By imagining things that could go wrong, you prepare your mind, reducing shock and highlighting your resilience.
